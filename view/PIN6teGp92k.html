Everything to Know About the 2023 BMW 7 Series / i7:

The seventh generation of BMW's 7-series flagship sedan, the i7, arrives for the 2023 model year with a polarizing design and a new all-electric variant. The i7 ushers in the future of full-size BMW luxury cruisers, with two electric motors producing 536 horsepower and a range of approximately 300 miles. However, BMW ensured that traditionalists were satisfied by offering a turbocharged 3.0-liter inline-six or twin-turbo 4.4-liter V-8 wrapped in a nearly identical design. The new 7-series and i7 also bring high-tech options like a 31-inch, 8K "Theater Screen" for the rear seats and a hands-free highway driver-assistance system. Our editors entered the world of fictitious money and specced their ideal BMW 7-series or i7 models now that the online configurator for both models is live.

Pricing And Which One To Buy:
BMW 740i: $268,900.
BMW i7 xDrive60: $297,900

The 740i will be powered by BMW's sweetly silken inline-six and will only be available with rear-wheel drive. Most buyers should find this version adequate, but for those seeking all-wheel drive or more horsepower, the 760i xDrive with a twin-turbo V-8 is available. We recommend the Executive Lounge package (power-adjustable reclining rear seats with massage, among other luxuries) and the cool 31.3-inch Theater Screen rear-seat entertainment system, which can stream content via the car's on-board Wi-Fi hotspot if you plan to be chauffeured in your new 7-series. The same body shell will be used for an all-electric variant called the i7, which we will look at separately.

Drivetrains:
The BMW 740i 2023 is powered by a 3.0-liter twin-turbo inline-six-cylinder petrol engine with a 48V mild-hybrid system, producing 280kW of power and 540Nm of torque. This is coupled to an eight-speed automatic transmission, with power only going to the rear wheels.
BMW claims the 740i can accelerate from 0 to 100 km/h in 5.4 seconds.
The i7 xDrive60 all-electric flagship uses a dual-motor all-wheel drive electric powertrain with total system outputs of 400kW and 745Nm. It connects to a 106kWh battery pack.
BMW claims the 0-100km/h sprint takes 4.7 seconds.

Efficiency:
BMW hasn’t detailed official consumption figures for either the 740i and i7 xDrive60.
The i7 xDrive60 has an electric range of up to 625km according to WLTP testing and has a maximum DC fast-charging speed of 195kW. BMW says the i7 xDrive60 can charge from 10 percent to 80 percent in 34 minutes.
The i7 xDrive60 also comes standard with a third-generation BMW Wallbox for home charging, as well as a complimentary five-year Chargefox subscription.

Dimensions:
The 2023 BMW 7 Series and i7 measure 5291mm long, 1950mm wide, and 1544mm tall with a 3215mm wheelbase.
Compared to the outgoing long-wheelbase model, the new range-topping sedan is 123mm longer, 48mm wider, 65mm taller, and has 5mm longer wheelbase. The 7 Series is now offered globally in a single length, instead of short- and long-wheelbase variants.

Interior:
The 7-series, as the pinnacle of the BMW lineup, provides its passengers with a multitude of luxuries. Rich-smelling leathers, stainless steel accents, and fancy glass and wood trim elements give the interior of the 7-series a posh feel; buyers can even select a new cashmere upholstery option, if that suits their personal aesthetic. The luxurious interior now has the technology, style, and opulence to compete with the Mercedes-Benz S-class. The 7-series impresses most when equipped with the Executive Lounge package, which includes power-adjustable reclining rear seats with massage, a wireless smartphone charging pad for the rear seat area, and adjustable neck cushions on the headrests. The trunk is about the size you'd expect for a full-size luxury car, and a rear-seat pass-through allows you to transport longer items like skis.

2023 BMW i7 xDrive60: All-Electric 7 Series:
The most compelling reason to live with the new 7 Series and its out-of-this-world styling is the all-electric i7 xDrive60. It has two electric motors (one in the front and one in the back) and a 101.7 kWh high-voltage battery that produces 536 horsepower and 549 lb-ft of torque, allowing it to accelerate from zero to 60 mph in 4.5 seconds. You won't be chasing Teslas and Lucids in your i7 xDrive60, but choosing a BMW in this case is about established luxury rather than raw power.
BMW anticipates a 300-mile range, which is reasonable for a vehicle of this size. An 11kW AC charger can be used to recharge the batteries, but the electric architecture accepts up to 195 kW of DC fast charging when needed.
